If B_t is Brownian motion and \overline{B}_t = \max_{0\le s\le t}B_s is the running max, then E[f(B_t) 1(\overline{B}_t > a)] = E[f(B_t) 1(B_t > a)] + E[f(2a - B_t) 1(B_t > a)]

Taking f(x) = 1 gives P(\overline{B}_t > a) = 2P(B_t > a).

1 - G(x) = 2(1 - F(x)), G(x) = 2F(x) - 1, x > 0.

g(x) = 2f(x), x > 0.

Let \tau_a = \inf\{t > 0\mid \overline{B}_t > a\} be the first time B

Note \tau_a < t if and only if \overline{B}_t > a.

Recall E[X] = \int_0^\infty P(X > x)\,dx if X is positive.

P(\min\{\tau_a, T\} > t) = P(\tau_a > t, T > t) = P(\tau_a > t)1(T > t)

E[\min\{\tau_a, T\}] = \int_0^T P(\tau_a > t)\,dt.

Let X_t = \mu t + \sigma B_t.

B_t - \alpha t is BM under P_\alpha where dP_\alpha/dP|_t = \exp(\alpha B_t - \alpha^2t/2).

\begin{aligned} &E[g(X_t) 1(\overline{X}_t > a)] \\ \qquad &= E[f(B_t) 1(\max_{0\le s\le t}\mu s + \sigma B_s > a)] \\ \qquad &= E[\exp(\alpha B_t - \alpha^2t/2) f(B_t - \alpha t) 1(\max_{0\le s\le t}\mu s + \sigma (B_s - \alpha s) > a)] \\ \qquad &= E[\exp((\mu/\sigma) B_t - (\mu/\sigma)^2t/2) f(B_t - (\mu/\sigma) t) 1(\max_{0\le s\le t}\sigma B_s > a)] \\ \qquad &= E[\exp((\mu/\sigma) B_t - (\mu/\sigma)^2t/2) f(B_t - (\mu/\sigma) t) 1(\max_{0\le s\le t}B_s > a/\sigma)] \\ \qquad &= E[h(B_t) 1(\overline{B}_t > a/\sigma)] \\ \qquad &= E[(h(B_t) + h(2a - B_t)) 1(B_t > a/\sigma)], \\ \end{aligned} where f(x) = g(\mu t + \sigma x) and h(x) = \exp((\mu/\sigma) x - (\mu/\sigma)^2t/2) f(x - (\mu/\sigma) t).

If g(x) = 1 then f(x) = 1 and h(x) = \exp((\mu/\sigma) x - (\mu/\sigma)^2t/2).

P(\overline{X}_t > a) = E[(\exp((\mu/\sigma) B_t - (\mu/\sigma)^2t/2) + \exp((\mu/\sigma) (2a - B_t) - (\mu/\sigma)^2t/2))1(B_t > a)].
