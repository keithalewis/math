Vector Space

Keith A. Lewis

June 29, 2023

Abstract
Classified by dimension

Let \mathit{F} be a field and I be a set. The set exponential \mathit{F}^I = \{x\colon I\to\mathit{R}\} is a vector space under pointwise addition and scalar multiplication, (x + y)(i) = x(i) + y(i) and (ax)(i) = ax(i), i\in I, x,y\in\mathit{F}^I, a\in\mathit{F}.

The standard basis is \{\delta^i\}_{i\in I} where \delta^i\in \mathit{F}^I is defined by \delta^i(j) = \delta_{ij}, the Kronecker delta function, for j\in I,

The vector space dual of \mathit{F}^I is \mathit{F}_I = (\mathit{F}^I)^* = \{x^*\colon \mathit{F}^I\to\mathit{F}\}. The standard dual basis is \{\delta_i\}_{i\in I} where \delta_i\in \mathit{F}_I is defined by \delta_i(\delta^j) = \delta_{ij}, j\in I,
