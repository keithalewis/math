Simple Universal Model

Based on Stephen Ross's 1978 paper "A Simple Approach to the Valuation of Risky Streams"

If there are no arbitrage opportunities in the capital markets, then there exists a (not generally unique) [linear] valuation operator, L.

Ross did say "(not generally unique)" but you have to read the paper to find out they are [linear]. If you have studied calculus, you know integration takes a function to a number linearly: the integral of a function times a constant is the constant times the integral, and the integral of a sum is the sum of the integrals.

Instruments have prices and pay associated cash flows after you buy them: bonds pay coupons, stocks pay dividends, futures have margin adjustments.
